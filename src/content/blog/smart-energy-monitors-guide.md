Your utility bill tells you *how much* electricity you used. A smart energy monitor tells you *what used it* — and that difference changes everything about how you save.

## What a smart energy monitor actually does

A monitor clips current sensors around the main feeds (and often individual circuits) inside your electrical panel. From there it streams live usage to an app, learning the signatures of your appliances over time.

## What it catches that your bill hides

Phantom loads, a failing fridge compressor, a water heater running longer than it should, an EV charger scheduled at the wrong rate window — these hide inside a monthly total but jump out instantly on a circuit-level graph.

## Whole-home vs circuit-level

Whole-home monitors (two sensors) are cheaper and simpler but rely on device-detection algorithms. Circuit-level systems put a sensor on every breaker for exact per-circuit numbers — more install effort, far better data.

## Why it matters more with an EV

Once an EV, heat pump, or battery joins your home, charging becomes your single biggest load. A monitor shows exactly what each charging session costs and whether your time-of-use scheduling is actually working.
