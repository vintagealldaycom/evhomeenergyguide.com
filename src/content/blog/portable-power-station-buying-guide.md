Portable power stations have exploded in variety — and marketing noise. Here's what actually matters.

## Three specs decide everything

**Capacity (Wh)** is how much energy it stores. **Output (W)** is how much it can deliver at once. **Chemistry** determines lifespan: LiFePO4 cells typically survive 3,000+ cycles versus roughly 500–800 for older NMC packs.

## Size it to your real loads

A fridge draws about 100–800Wh per day depending on efficiency. A CPAP machine runs 30–60W. A laptop charge is roughly 60Wh. Add up your actual devices before falling for a spec sheet.

## Solar input is where cheap units cut corners

Look at the maximum solar input wattage. A 2kWh station that only accepts 200W of solar takes 10+ hours of perfect sun to refill. Better units accept 400–800W.
