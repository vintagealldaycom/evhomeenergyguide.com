Tool batteries are often half the cost of the tool. A few habits can double how long they last.

## Store at partial charge, not full

Lithium-ion cells age fastest when stored full or empty. If a pack will sit unused for weeks, leave it around 40–60% charged. Many modern chargers have a "storage" mode that does this for you.

## Heat is the real killer

A battery left on a truck dashboard in summer degrades more in one afternoon than in months of normal use. Store packs indoors, away from direct sun, and let a hot pack cool before charging it.

## Cold weather rules

Cold doesn't permanently damage packs, but never charge a battery below freezing — bring it inside first. Runtime drops in the cold and recovers when the pack warms up.

## Myths worth ignoring

You don't need to fully discharge modern lithium packs (that was nickel-cadmium advice from decades ago), and leaving a pack on a quality charger overnight occasionally is fine — modern chargers stop at full.
