Adding an EV charger, heat pump, or battery to an older home often triggers the dreaded question: does the panel need an upgrade?

## What a smart panel does

A smart panel replaces or augments your breaker box with circuit-level monitoring and control. You can see exactly what every circuit uses in real time, and — critically — automatically shed low-priority loads when the panel approaches its limit.

## Load management can save you thousands

A 100-amp service upgrade can cost $2,000–$10,000+ depending on your utility. Load management devices let a 100A panel safely support an EV charger by pausing charging when the dryer and oven run, then resuming automatically.

## The main players

Span, Schneider's Square D Energy Center, and simpler dedicated load-sharing relays all approach this differently — full panel replacement at one end, a single smart breaker at the other.

## Where to start

Before buying anything, get a week of usage data. Many utilities show hourly usage in your online account, which tells you how much headroom your panel really has.
