Vehicle-to-load turns your EV's battery into a quiet, fume-free generator. A weekend of camp power typically uses less range than you'd think.

## What V2L actually provides

Most V2L-equipped EVs supply 1.5–3.6kW of 120V AC power through an adapter that plugs into the charge port, or built-in outlets in the cabin or bed.

## A realistic camp power budget

An electric cooler (40W), string lights (10W), induction cooktop bursts (1,500W for 30 min/day), phone and laptop charging — a full day of comfortable camping usually totals 2–4kWh. On a 75kWh pack, that's about 3–5% of your battery per day, or roughly 10–15 miles of range.

## Climate mode changes the math

Sleeping in the car with climate control running draws far more — typically 8–15% of the pack overnight depending on outside temperature. Plan your return-trip range accordingly.

## Don't forget the adapter

V2L adapters are brand-specific. Buy the official one or a well-reviewed certified equivalent; this is not the place for a no-name bargain.
